    Coffee seeds according to their geographical origin

    Colombian seeds are very bitter, with a strong smell and a bit acidic. On the other hand, the Brazilian seeds are softer and sweeter, the Mexican ones are slightly acidic and with little aroma, and the Costa Rican ones have an intense flavor. African seeds are native to Tanzania, Kenya and Ethiopia. All of them have a high acid level, strong flavor and aroma. Finally, the Asian seeds come from Java, Yemen and Celebes. While the seeds of Java are characterized by being very bitter, those of Yemen have a great chocolate flavor and those of Celebes are very acid and strong in flavor.
